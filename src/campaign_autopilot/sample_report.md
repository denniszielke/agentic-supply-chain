# Weekly Competitor & Margin Briefing

## Executive summary

Competitive pressure this week is concentrated in **dairy & eggs**, **meat &
sausage** and **fruit & vegetables**, where discounters are running deep
headline cuts on staples. Holding our current shelf prices on the hero lines
risks visible basket-trip loss to the price-sensitive *Value-Driven Family*,
while a blanket match would erode weekly margin on high-volume SKUs. The
recommendations below match competitors selectively on traffic-driving heroes,
protect each category P&L with full-margin anchors, and keep every promo price
above the cost floor. Net forecast impact across the three categories is
**flat-to-accretive weekly margin** with an estimated **+6.1% incremental
footfall** on the targeted personas.

## Key risks

- **Perishability** — strawberries (4-day shelf life) and chicken breast
  (5 days) need tight volume control; over-ordering converts margin into waste.
- **Competitor counter-move** — a deeper rival cut on butter could force a
  second markdown; keep €0.10 of headroom above the cost floor on AS-ME-005.
- **Cost-floor proximity** — the chicken-breast footfall play runs close to the
  procurement-plus-logistics floor; do not extend it beyond one week.
- **Persona cannibalisation** — the Premium Foodie anchors assume no spillover
  discount; avoid stacking coupons on the held lines.
